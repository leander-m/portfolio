Intro

A design system case study about scaling Rapid7's product foundations from Figma components into coded libraries, platform patterns and AI-assisted prototyping workflows. The work focused on making UI more consistent, customisable and production-ready across a large cybersecurity platform.

Rapid7's design system needed to support a wide range of products, workflows and acquired experiences, while still feeling like one coherent platform. The work began with foundations: defining a token architecture that could carry Rapid7's visual language across design and code, and create a more flexible base for theming, component customisation and long-term platform consistency.

A major shift was moving away from a fully in-house component approach and towards wrapping an existing library, MUI, with Rapid7's own design language. This meant using the token structure to customise the library at a deep level, bringing Rapid7's look and feel into coded components while still benefiting from the reliability, accessibility and engineering maturity of an established framework.

As the system grew, the work became less about individual components and more about governance, scale and product fit. Components needed to work across many different product areas, support dense cybersecurity workflows and remain usable as new teams and acquired products entered the platform. This involved large waves of refinement, review and customisation to make sure the system could flex without fragmenting.

Over time, the design system matured into a broader platform foundation. Alongside components and tokens, it began to include documented templates, reusable patterns and experience-level guidance. This helped teams move from solving the same layout and interaction problems in slightly different ways to working from a shared set of standards.

More recently, the role of the design system has shifted again with AI-assisted prototyping. As AI became more useful for generating coded prototypes, the design system became a key guardrail for quality. I created a prototyping setup that pulls together the required Rapid7 packages and repositories, then adds rules, steering and custom agents to help generated output stay aligned to the design system. The goal was not just faster prototypes, but better ones: consistent, recognisable and closer to the quality expected from the platform.

This project shows how a design system can become more than a component library. It can act as the shared foundation for design, engineering, governance and AI-assisted workflows, helping teams move faster while protecting the craft and consistency of the product.
